The unsolved matchday problem

Every command center in the world assumes the network survives kickoff. It doesn't.

We studied the platforms being deployed for 2026 — Lenovo's stadium digital twins, FIFA's Football AI Pro, and a wave of hackathon "GenAI copilots." They're genuinely good at prediction. They all share the same blind spot.

01 · Infrastructure reality

A stadium is a small city that melts its own network.

A single match can push past 50 terabytes of data through one venue, and reconnection storms after a goal can cascade into the "avalanche effect" — the exact moment your AI dashboard needs a live signal is the moment 60–80,000 phones are fighting for the same cell tower.

02 · One brain, one point of failure

A single chat copilot isn't an operations team.

Existing GenAI stadium concepts route every question — wayfinding, medical, translation, sustainability — through one generic model and one dashboard. When it's confidently wrong or simply overloaded, every function degrades together.

03 · Accessibility as an afterthought

"Wheelchair gate reserved" isn't accessibility design.

Sensory-sensitive fans, Deaf and hard-of-hearing fans, blind fans, and fans managing anxiety or neurodivergence need fundamentally different journeys — not one extra rule bolted onto a crowd-routing model.

The architecture

Meet the Mesh — eight agents, one Conductor

Instead of one model trying to do everything, SYNAPSE runs eight purpose-built GenAI agents that specialize, argue, and hand off to each other — orchestrated by a Conductor that explains every decision in plain language.

Agent 01

Navigation

Turn-by-turn wayfinding to seat, gate, restroom or concession, re-routed live from crowd density — cached on-device so it still works with zero bars.

Edge-first
Agent 02

Crowd Dynamics

Forecasts density 8–15 minutes ahead per gate and concourse using anonymized flow signals, not facial recognition — flags bottlenecks before they form.

Predictive
Agent 03

Accessibility

Builds a personal "Fan Twin" journey per access need — sensory-friendly routing, Deaf-alert visuals, audio-haptic wayfinding for blind fans, quiet-room booking.

Personalized
Agent 04

Transit & Transport

Blends metro, rideshare, shuttle and parking telemetry into one departure-time recommendation per fan, tuned to their gate and accessibility profile.

Multi-modal
Agent 05

Sustainability

Scores each fan's real journey footprint and turns it into live, personalized incentives — priority lanes, transit credit, merchandise — not a static dashboard.

Incentive loop
Agent 06

Multilingual Dispatch

Live voice and sign-supported translation across 40+ languages for fans, and instant on-shift briefings for volunteers who don't share a language with a visitor.

40+ languages
Agent 07

Operational Intelligence

Rolls up all 16 venues into one live briefing for organizers, with a plain-language "why" behind every recommendation — built for audits, not just dashboards.

Explainable
Agent 08

Decision Support & Emergency Triage

Fuses camera, radio and volunteer voice reports into a drafted incident brief for human responders — always human-confirmed, never an autonomous dispatch.
